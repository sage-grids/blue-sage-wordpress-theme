# Customizing Your Site's Look

Blue Sage is a "Block Theme," which means you can customize its appearance directly within WordPress using the **Site Editor**.

## 1. Entering the Site Editor
To start customizing your site:
1. Go to **Appearance > Editor**.
2. Click anywhere on the preview of your site to enter the editor.

## 2. Using Global Styles
You can change the colors, fonts, and layout of your entire site in one place.
1. In the Site Editor, click the **Styles (Half-Circle) Icon** in the top-right corner.
2. From here, you can:
   - **Change Colors:** Update your site's primary palette (e.g., change "Rich Blue" to your brand's specific blue).
   - **Update Typography:** Change the fonts and sizes for your headings and body text.
   - **Adjust Spacing:** Control the default gaps between your blocks.

## 3. Changing Your Logo
Your site's logo is usually located in the **Header** (the top section of your site).
1. Click on the logo image in the editor.
2. Click the **Replace** button to upload your own logo.
3. You can also adjust the logo's width in the block settings on the right.

## 4. Editing Templates
Templates define the overall layout of different types of pages (like your single posts or your home page).
- In the Editor, click **Templates** in the left sidebar.
- Select a template to edit it. For example, you can change the layout of your **Front Page** or your **Single Post** template.

## 5. Saving Your Changes
When you're finished customizing, don't forget to click the **Save** button in the top-right corner. WordPress will show you exactly which parts of your site you're saving.
